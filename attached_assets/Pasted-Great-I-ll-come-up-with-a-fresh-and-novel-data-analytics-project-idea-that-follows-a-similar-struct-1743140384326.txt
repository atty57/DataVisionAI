## Objectives
This project aims to answer several key questions and provide insights into the EV adoption landscape:
- **Growth Trends:** How have EV sales grown over the past decade? Identify year-over-year growth rates and major inflection points (e.g. when did exponential growth begin).  
- **Regional Leaders and Laggards:** Which countries or regions are leading in electric vehicle adoption, and which are falling behind? For instance, compare EV uptake in markets like China, Europe, the US, and others to highlight disparities.  
- **Market Share Analysis:** What share of new vehicle sales do EVs account for over time, and how close are we to EVs overtaking conventional cars in certain markets? This includes examining places with exceptionally high EV market share (like Norway) to understand what sets them apart.  
- **Adoption Patterns:** Examine patterns such as whether EV adoption is accelerating yearly and if there are any seasonal or policy-driven spikes (if monthly or quarterly data is available). - **Infrastructure vs. Adoption (Advanced):** If data is available, assess whether the growth in charging infrastructure is keeping pace with the growth in EV ownership. Do areas with more charging stations per capita see higher EV adoption?  
- **Forecasting Future Adoption (Advanced):** Using historical trends, project future EV sales or market share – for example, what might the global EV sales in 2030 look like if current growth continues? Explore scenarios such as reaching 50% of new sales or more.  
- **Clustering and Segmentation (Advanced):** Employ clustering to group countries or cities by their EV adoption trajectories or characteristics. For instance, cluster nations into categories like “early adopters,” “fast followers,” and “late adopters” based on their adoption rate and growth curves.

By exploring these objectives, the project will not only characterize the current state of EV adoption but also derive insights into the factors driving it and the challenges ahead.
